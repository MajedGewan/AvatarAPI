### **The Law of the Book of Justice**

**Chapter One**  
*Definitions*

**Article (1):**  
The following words and phrases shall have the meanings indicated next to each of them:  
**Ministry:** Ministry of Justice  
**Minister:** Minister of Justice

---

**Chapter Two**  
*Appointment of Notaries and their Spatial Jurisdiction*

**Article (2):**  
The notary public is the director of his department, and if there are multiple notaries in one department, the most senior will manage the department.

**Article (3):**  
A notary public is required to fulfill general employment conditions as stipulated by the Civil Service Law, have a university degree in Sharia or law from a recognized university or college, and must not have any penal or disciplinary rulings affecting his reputation or honor.

**Article (4):**  
Before assuming duties, the notary public shall take the following oath: "I swear by God Almighty that I will perform my work with honesty, honor, and trustworthiness, that I will preserve job-related secrets, and respect the laws," in the presence of the Minister or his delegate.

**Article (5):**  
An official seal shall be allocated to each department of the notary public. The department director shall receive it and be responsible for it.

---

**Chapter Three**  
*Competencies and Prohibitions*

**Article (6):**  
The notary public shall draft contracts within his competence, certify signatures, and prove the date of customary documents, as stipulated by law.

**Article (7):**  
The notary public shall draft contracts requested by law or the concerned parties and record them in special records without any erasure or deletion. Corrections shall be done in red ink and in the presence of the interested parties. The notary must keep the original documents and provide copies upon request.

**Article (8):**  
The notary public shall authenticate the signatures of the concerned parties in private documents upon request, recording the authentication in the registers and special indexes.

**Article (9):**  
Before drafting contracts or authenticating signatures, the notary must verify the identity of the concerned parties with official documents or two identifiers. If the subject matter is contrary to public order, the notary must refuse to draft the document or authenticate signatures and refer the matter to the primary court for approval.

**Article (10):**  
If the notary does not understand the language of the concerned parties, a translator or expert must be involved to facilitate understanding. The translator or expert must take an oath to perform their duty honestly. This process must be recorded and signed by all parties involved.

**Article (11):**  
For parties unable to sign, the notary shall take a thumbprint and record it in the document.

**Article (12):**  
If a document spans multiple sheets, the notary must number the pages, note the number of pages, and seal and sign each sheet with the parties involved.

**Article (13):**  
The notary shall record the date of customary documents with consecutive numbers and provide the interested party with a certificate of the document's entry date.

**Article (14):**  
Upon request, the notary public shall prepare marriage documents and divorce certificates as per the Minister’s decision.

**Article (15):**  
The Minister shall specify records and indexes that the notary must maintain, as well as procedures for keeping original documents.

**Article (16):**  
The notary is prohibited from drafting or authenticating signatures on documents where laws assign this responsibility to another entity, including real estate transactions, sales, and gifts without proper authorization. Additionally, the notary must not perform any action involving personal benefit for himself or his family or disclose confidential information.

---

**Chapter Four**  
*General Provisions*

**Article (17):**  
All documents drafted by the notary must be written in Arabic. Documents in other languages may only be authenticated after legal translation into Arabic.

**Article (18):**  
The Minister shall issue decisions specifying fees for contract drafting, signature authentication, and other services.

**Article (19):**  
Transactions requested by official authorities shall be exempt from fees.

**Royal Decree No. 74/3 Issuing the Commercial Registry Law**  
**We, Qaboos bin Said, Sultan of Oman**  
Based on the presentation by the Minister of Development regarding issuing a Commercial Registry Law, we decided as follows:

**Article 1**: A Secretariat of the Commercial Registry shall be established in the Ministry of Development, tasked with maintaining a registry called the Commercial Registry, where the information stipulated in this law regarding traders and companies mentioned in Article 4 below is recorded. The number of employees in the Secretariat of the Commercial Registry and their respective functions shall be determined by Royal Decree. The Secretary of the Commercial Registry must take an oath before the Minister of Development to perform his duties faithfully and sincerely.

**Article 2**: The public has the right to access the information recorded in the Commercial Registry, and anyone may obtain a copy of this information or part of it for a specific fee. The Commercial Registry serves as a means of publication and acts as conclusive evidence of the information recorded therein when expressly provided by law.

**Article 3**: The Secretariat of the Commercial Registry in the capital is the central authority of the Commercial Registry. The Minister of Development may decide to establish regional Commercial Registry Secretariats in other regions by decision. Each regional Secretariat shall maintain a registry for its respective region and send a copy of all the information recorded to the central authority for inclusion in the central Commercial Registry.

**Article 5**: The following must be registered in the Commercial Registry:
1. Traders whose principal place of business is in Oman.
2. Commercial companies whose principal place of business is in Oman.
3. Branches and agencies established in Oman by traders or commercial companies whose principal place of business is abroad.
4. Branches and agencies established in Oman by traders or commercial companies whose principal place of business is registered in Oman in an area that does not administratively cover those branches or agencies.
5. Legally established traders, commercial companies, branches, and agencies operating in Oman and engaged in commercial activities as of the publication date of this law in the Official Gazette.

**Article 6**: A trader is considered anyone whose profession is to engage in commercial activities. A commercial company is defined as any legal entity engaged in commercial activities.  
The following are considered commercial activities and similar activities aimed at profit:
1. Buying movable or immovable property, tangible or intangible, for the purpose of selling it for profit, whether sold in its original state or after modification.
2. Buying or renting such properties for the purpose of renting them to others.
3. Selling, renting, or subleasing purchased or leased properties as described above.
4. Banking or financial activities.
5. Manufacturing activities.
6. Land and maritime transport operations and storage services.
7. Labor and brokerage transactions.
8. Insurance services.
9. Hotel, restaurant, and entertainment services.
10. Publishing and printing activities.
11. The exploitation of petroleum, gas, and mineral resources.
12. Real estate construction and development activities.
13. Commercial agency activities.

**Article 6**: Individuals engaged in agriculture, fishing, small trades, simple crafts, or small transportation operations on land or water with minimal general expenses are exempt from registration in the Commercial Registry.  
The Ministry of Development has the authority to exempt any category of traders or commercial companies from registration in the Commercial Registry.

---

**Chapter Two: Registration of Traders Whose Principal Place of Business is in Oman**

**Article 7**: Every trader whose principal place of business is in Oman must request registration in the Commercial Registry Secretariat corresponding to that location within one month of commencing work. The trader must submit a declaration in duplicate, signed by them, containing:
1. Their name and surname.
2. The name under which they conduct business, if any.
3. Place and date of birth.
4. The date and manner in which they acquired Omani nationality.
5. Type of business.
6. Address of the principal place of business.
7. Addresses of branches or agencies, whether inside or outside Oman.
8. The shop's logo, if any.
9. Name and surname of each person authorized to sign and the scope of their authority.
10. Name and type of previous or current business activities conducted by the declarant.

**Article 8**: The Secretary of the Registry shall record the contents of the declaration in the Commercial Registry and provide the trader with a validated copy, showing the registration number.  
Additionally, the following must be registered in the Commercial Registry within one month of occurrence:
1. Any modification or change to the originally registered information.
2. Judgments and decisions imposing or lifting seizures on the trader.
3. Judgments and decisions declaring the trader's bankruptcy or related to bankruptcy or those ending the state of bankruptcy or restoring credit.
4. The sale or transfer of the business.

---

**Chapter Three: Registration of Commercial Companies Whose Principal Place of Business is in Oman**

**Article 9**: Every commercial company whose principal place of business is in Oman must be registered in the Commercial Registry of the region corresponding to that location. The company's directors or board members must request registration within one month of the company's formation. Applicants for registration must submit to the Commercial Registry a copy of the company's Articles of Association or incorporation contract, signed according to regulations by the board members or partners, along with a declaration in duplicate, signed by the applicants, containing the following:
1. Company name and type.
2. Company purpose.
3. Principal place of business and addresses of branches and agencies, both inside and outside Oman.
4. The name, surname, nationality, date, and place of birth of each partner, except for joint-stock companies, where this information is only required for board members.
5. Name and surname of each person authorized to sign on behalf of the company and the extent of their authority.
6. Company capital and the estimated value of any contributions to capital, whether in kind or services.
7. The date of company formation and, if applicable, the date of expiration.
8. Date and number of the Ministry of Development's license, if the company includes one or more foreign partners in accordance with the Foreign Capital Investment Law.

**Article 10**: The following must also be registered in the Commercial Registry within one month of occurrence:
1. Any modification or change to the originally registered information.
2. Judgments and decisions dissolving or annulling the company.
3. Judgments and decisions declaring the company's bankruptcy or related to bankruptcy or those ending the state of bankruptcy or restoring credit.
4. The sale, transfer, or merger of the company with another.

---

**Chapter Four: Registration of Branches and Agencies**

**Article 11**: Every trader or company with a principal place of business abroad and a branch or agency in Oman must register their branch or agency in the Commercial Registry Secretariat of the region where the branch or agency is located.  
Likewise, every trader or company with a principal place of business in Oman and a branch or agency in a region different from the one where the principal place of business is located must register their branch or agency in the Commercial Registry Secretariat of the region where the branch or agency is located.  
The registration described in this article must be completed for traders according to the procedures outlined in Articles 7 and 8 and for companies according to the procedures outlined in Articles 9 and 10 of this law. The person responsible for the branch or agency must submit the declaration and required information, including the name and surname of each person authorized to sign on behalf of the branch or agency and the extent of their authority.

---

**Chapter Five: Registration of Traders, Companies, Branches, and Agencies Operating in Oman on the Publication Date of This Law**

**Article 12**: The provisions of this law apply to traders, commercial companies, branches, and agencies legally established in Oman and engaged in commercial activities as of the publication date of this law in the Official Gazette. They must be registered in the Commercial Registry within six months from the effective date of this law.

---

**Chapter Six: Miscellaneous Provisions**

**Article 13**: Every applicant for registration must deposit with the Commercial Registry a sample of the signature of each person authorized to sign on their behalf, specifying whether the person is authorized to sign individually or only jointly with others. The applicant must also specify whether the authority is limited to a specific amount or transaction.  
Signature samples must be submitted with the initial registration application, and any modifications must be registered in the Commercial Registry.

**Article 14**: The contents of the Commercial Registry concerning persons authorized to sign on behalf of the registrant and their respective powers constitute conclusive evidence of the existence and scope of such powers when relied upon by third parties acting in good faith.

**Article 15**: If a trader dies, ceases commercial activities, or if a company is liquidated, or if a branch or agency is closed, the registration pertaining to the trader, company, branch, or agency must be deleted from the Commercial Registry. The trader, their heirs, the liquidator, or the company must submit a deletion request within one month of the event. The Secretary of the Commercial Registry also has the right to carry out the deletion on their own initiative.

**Article 16**: The Commercial Registry Secretariat may only refuse registration if the declaration submitted does not include all the required information and documents under this law or if the required fee has not been paid.

**Article 17**: The registration number and location in the Commercial Registry must be mentioned on all official correspondence, invoices, and other documents issued by registered natural or legal persons.

**Article 18**: Anyone required by law to submit an application or document to the Commercial Registry Secretariat and fails to do so within the legally specified period, as well as any natural or legal person registered in the Commercial Registry who violates the provisions of the previous article, shall be fined between twenty-five and two hundred Omani Riyals. The fine is doubled in case of repetition.
